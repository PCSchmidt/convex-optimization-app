Introduction to Convex Optimization

Convex optimization is a subfield of mathematical optimization that studies the problem of minimizing convex functions over convex sets. The importance of convex optimization lies in its wide applicability and the existence of efficient algorithms for solving these problems.

Key Concepts:

  • Convex Set: A set \(C\) is convex if for any two points in \(C\), the line segment connecting them is also in \(C\).
  • Convex Function: A function \(f\) is convex if its epigraph (the set of points above the graph of the function) is a convex set.
  • Objective Function: The function we aim to minimize or maximize in an optimization problem.
  • Constraints: Conditions that the solution must satisfy, often defining the feasible set.
  • Optimal Solution: The point in the feasible set that minimizes (or maximizes) the objective function.
